# Our Approach Page — Source Truth

## Page role

`our-approach.html` explains **why MediMotive’s way of working fits real manufacturing problems** — not **what proprietary tools or audits MediMotive runs internally**.

The page supports credibility and routes visitors to Expertise and Case Studies. It is not a method manual.

## Positioning rule (highest priority)

**Lead with the problem. End with the kind of solution and where proof lives.**

| Do | Do not |
|----|--------|
| Name situations visitors recognise (supplier switch without spec, ramp-up chaos, QMS that shop floor works around, OEM escalation) | Name branded frameworks as product features (Interaction Matrix, Process Purpose Audit, etc.) |
| Describe outcomes (failure path visible, repeatable action, fewer repeat complaints) | Walk through step-by-step “how we do it” |
| Point to case studies and expertise areas | Sound like revealing IP or selling a certification course |
| Use plain manufacturing language | Use “proprietary philosophy,” “developed by,” or consultant-theatre |

## Approved content structure

Use this narrative order when rewriting or auditing the page:

1. **Hero — problem landscape**  
   Most expensive failures sit between departments, suppliers, documentation, and real production behaviour — not in a single isolated mistake.

2. **Three problem → solution themes** (rename cards to outcomes, not tool names)  
   Suggested public themes (titles may be edited for tone, but must stay outcome-led):

   - **When departments do not see each other’s consequences** — purchasing, engineering, production, and quality make locally “correct” decisions that create downstream failure. *Solution direction:* map interfaces and decisions before they become field complaints or scrap.

   - **When the system exists on paper but not at the machine** — workers work around procedures; audits see documents, not behaviour. *Solution direction:* align process purpose with what actually happens on the shop floor.

   - **When evidence starts at the workbench** — diagnosis from physical reality (part, measurement, station), communicated upward so management can act. *Solution direction:* shop-floor pragmatism tied to clear corrective action.

3. **Proof bridge**  
   Case studies show what happens when interfaces are ignored vs managed — without disclosing confidential method detail (same boundary as case-study pages).

4. **Closing — practical promise**  
   Restate brand promise: understand the real process, find the real failure path, turn problems into practical repeatable action. CTA to Expertise and Case Studies.

## Hero copy rules

- **H1:** Our Approach (unchanged URL and nav label)
- **Eyebrow:** Avoid “proprietary,” “developed by,” or years-of-method marketing. Prefer neutral context, e.g. `How MediMotive works in practice` or `Working philosophy`.
- **Lead:** One or two short paragraphs on **problems at interfaces** and **why disconnected cost-centre thinking fails** — not “treating the company as a living organism” or methodology exposition.

## Banned on this page (unless client explicitly approves)

- Section headings that are internal method/product names
- Percentage claims without source (“80% of failures…”)
- “Textbook [Method Name] failure” labels on case cards
- Pills/tags that advertise method names
- Long third-person explanation of proprietary audits
- Copy that implies the visitor can replicate the full method from the page alone

## Links from other pages

Homepage, Services, About, and footer may link to Our Approach with **problem- or outcome-led** teaser lines, for example:

- “How MediMotive connects shop-floor reality with management decisions”
- “Why interface failures drive most production quality cost”

Avoid teasers like “Read the Interaction Matrix” or “Learn our proprietary method.”

## Homepage “Organic Connectivity” section

The homepage chapter label **Organic Connectivity** may remain for navigation continuity. Panel copy there should stay **problem- and principle-led** (interfaces between functions), not method reveal. Link text: **Our Approach →** (not “Organic Quality Method”).

## SEO / meta

Meta descriptions should mention **practical manufacturing quality** and **connected production problems**, not “proprietary philosophy” or internal framework names.
