# Voice and Banned Patterns

## Voice

MediMotive should sound:

- practical
- friendly
- fair
- direct
- calm
- technically credible
- no-nonsense
- human
- clear
- trustworthy
- grounded in real manufacturing work

## Preferred Language

Use phrases such as:

- practical quality work
- close to production
- real process
- shop-floor reality
- supplier communication
- regulatory expectations
- management decisions
- real failure path
- practical, repeatable action
- process stability
- transition security
- organic connectivity
- quality problems affect cost, delivery, trust, and company value

## Avoid

Avoid:

- academic fluff
- consultant theatre
- paper-heavy audits
- generic business optimization
- colorful charts
- corporate transformation language
- vague buzzwords
- aggressive “book now” language
- overusing “I”
- personal CV tone
- stock-photo agency tone

## Banned or Risky Patterns

Do not use:

- “We optimize your business”
- “Digital transformation”
- “Agile excellence”
- “World-class solutions”
- “Book a call now”
- “Request a Shop-Floor Check” unless the website returns to active consulting funnel mode
- “Gallery” in the main nav
- “Home” in the main nav
- fake logo or “MM” icon
- “paperless transformation”
- “one-stop-shop”
- “best-in-class” without proof
- proprietary method names on public pages (e.g. Interaction Matrix, Process Purpose Audit as headings)
- “proprietary working philosophy” / “our method reveals” / step-by-step methodology marketing on Our Approach
- teasers that invite copying an internal framework without engagement

## Our Approach — voice (problem → solution)

When writing or editing `our-approach.html` or teasers that link to it:

- Lead with **manufacturing problems** the visitor recognises (escalation, ramp-up, supplier dispute, paper vs shop floor, transition risk).
- Describe **solution direction** (clear failure path, practical action, stability, fewer repeat failures) — not proprietary method steps.
- Route to **Expertise** and **Case Studies** for proof.

Do not present Our Approach as method disclosure. Full rules: [11-our-approach-page.md](11-our-approach-page.md).

## Writing Rule

Every section must communicate one of:

- what MediMotive stands for
- how MediMotive thinks
- what proof exists
- why Björn is credible
- what practical manufacturing problem is being addressed
- what changes for the visitor when the problem is addressed (solution outcome), especially on Our Approach

## Tone Balance

MediMotive should feel confident but not arrogant.

Björn should feel approachable but not casual.

The site should be professional but not cold.

## Approved Human Tone

The About page may sound warm, practical, and approachable.

Approved founder section tone:

- practical, fair, and straightforward
- calm under pressure
- helpful without being soft
- no-bullshit without sounding rude
- technically credible without sounding arrogant

Do not remove the friendly human layer in the name of making the site more industrial.
